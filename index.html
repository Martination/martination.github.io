About Me

I am a junior Computer Science major at Carleton College in beautiful, rural Northfield, Minnesota - the city of Cows, Colleges, and Contentment (I'm serious). I'm an American/British dual-national from the north end of Los Angeles in beautiful, suburban La Cañada, California.

I've had or been around computers for as long as I can remember. When I was young, I was really good at breaking them. Now, I still break things, but that tends to be code rather than drivers. All that tinkering has instilled in me a sense of curiosity and intrigue in technology, science, and the broader world, in a How It's Made kind of way.

On campus, I work as a Media Technology Assistant at PEPS (Presentation, Event, and Production Support) where I support campus events with microphones, projectors, or laptops, check out equipment to students, staff, or faculty so they can run their own events or projects, and record and edit larger-scale events. Think of it like AV club. Additionally, I pick up extra jobs at PEPS that need doing, such as scheduling shifts for other students, organizing and assigning tickets via our internal system, or setting up Raspberry Pi's to loop videos for an installation in our library.

Some class projects that I'm particularly proud of include

  • A 3D realtime graphics engine — both a from-scratch engine in C starting from a basic triangle rasterizer up to 3D rendering with lights and camera controls, and an OpenGL hardware engine which includes shadow mapping and shaders
  • An interactive Scheme interpreter built in C
  • A 2D player-vs-player fighting game built in JavaFX
  • A dynamically built website for searching for alternative fuel source stations across the United States (such as ethanol, electric, or hydrogen) built with Flask and PostgreSQL
