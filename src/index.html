Martin Green

I am a recent graduate of Carleton College in beautiful, rural Northfield, Minnesota - the city of Cows, Colleges, and Contentment (I'm serious) with a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science. I'm an American/British dual-national from the north end of Los Angeles in beautiful, suburban La Cañada, California.

I've had or been around computers for as long as I can remember. When I was young, I was really good at breaking them. Now, I still break things, but that tends to be code rather than drivers. All that tinkering has instilled in me a sense of curiosity and intrigue in technology, science, and the broader world, in a How It's Made kind of way. If you get to know me, you will quickly learn that I like space and astronomy, security, live event setup, and renewable energy.

Now I work as an Audio Visual Technician at PSAV in which I utilize my aforementioned technical background to setup events large and small, work with clients on any pop-up requests or problems, and assist third-party companies with their own setups. We strive for excellent customer service while delivering a high-quality experience. I am regularly experimenting with our equipment to better learn each device's nuances and abilities so that when the time comes, I will be ready to use it at a moment's notice.

At Carleton, I worked as a Student Team Lead at PEPS (Presentation, Events, and Production Support) under the Information Technology Services department where I helped to manage other student staff with schedules, assignments, and training, support campus events with equipment such as microphones or projectors, check out equipment, and record and edit larger-scale events. Think of it like AV club. Additionally, I picked up anything that was needed at PEPS, such as developing our own system for using Raspberry Pi's to loop videos for installations across campus. In this role, I was often working with campus community members from faculty to admissions, advising them on equipment recommendations and proper setup.

Personally and academically, there are a few projects that I'm particularly proud of:

  • An in-browser photo editor called Carmen, using the CamanJS library, React, and Bootstrap.
  • A tool to verify a person's COVID-19 vaccination status (Verify Vax) using SMART Health Cards and associated QR codes, such as the ones from the State of California or State of Louisiana.
  • My senior thesis project of developing an app to visualize on-campus energy data and engage community members in reducing our carbon footprint
  • A simulation of wind turbines to better optimize placement in a wind farm, based on simulation data using the Lattice-Boltzmann algorithm
  • A visualization of historical and projected energy use in the US broken down by fuel type built using the D3 JavaScript library
  • A 3D realtime graphics engine — both from-scratch using C starting from a basic rasterizer up to 3D lighting and rendering, and an OpenGL hardware engine which includes shadow mapping and shaders
  • An interactive Scheme interpreter built in C
  • A 2D player-vs-player fighting game built in JavaFX
  • A dynamically built website for searching for alternative fuel source stations across the United States (such as ethanol, electric, or hydrogen) built with Flask and PostgreSQL
